Why Self-Guided Works for Falls Park

Falls Park is compact — just 1.2 miles of walking — which makes it ideal for a self-guided experience. The park's paths are clearly marked, the stops are close together, and you can't really get lost. What you gain is freedom: the ability to linger at the waterfall, grab coffee on Main Street, and actually absorb the stories without being rushed.

What Our Tour Covers

10 historic stops with professional narration covering Cherokee heritage, the Liberty Bridge's engineering secrets, Greenville's textile empire, Duke's Mayonnaise origins, a Nobel Prize-winning inventor, and the remarkable transformation of Falls Park from a hidden, polluted site to the city's front porch.

How GPS-Triggered Audio Works

As you walk, your phone's GPS detects when you're near each stop. The audio starts automatically — no buttons to press, no map to follow. Just walk and listen. If GPS isn't available, you can also tap any stop manually on the interactive map.
